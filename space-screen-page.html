The Space Screen

Visit Devpost Page

Details

The ‘Space Screen’ was a project created for the ‘To the Moon and Hack’ hackathon, hosted by Major League Hacking (MLH). To learn more about the project, its challenges, and its inner-workings, please visit the Devpost page using the link provided above.

This aim of this project was to allow for astronauts out on spacewalks to be much more independent and be able to view important pieces of data such as their oxygen levels, and suit temperature value on their own without it needing to be conveyed by an external person. Fundamentally, the ‘Space Screen’ is a watch-like device that could be put on the non-dominant arm of the spacesuit, it would be responsible for monitoring all vital pieces of information regarding the space suit and then display those values on the LCD panel. The astronaut could then just check all his stats themselves by just looking at the screen, therefore removing the need for another person having to constantly report it to them and thus making the entire process more simple and efficient all while giving the astronaut in the suit more control.

A functioning prototype circuit of how the device would function was created using an LCD panel, breadboard, Arduino Uno, and some jumper cables along with code in C++. The LCD panel would showcase the pieces of information the astronaut would require in the form of percentages.
