Ishpreet Nagi

Aspiring programmer

Student. Programmer. Leader.

Emerging from Brampton, I am an aspiring programmer, and currently a Honour's computer science student (co-op) at McMaster University. My first exposure to the marvels and capabilities of computer science, was in grade 9 where during the course of a Python-based elective I had taken at my high school, I got the chance to develop a small arcade game. Through its creation process, I fell in love with the problem-solving and the puzzle-like nature of programming, as well as the freedom and power it rewards one with which allows them to not only create momentous applications but give way to innovations that further the landscape as a whole.

Throughout my time at McMaster University, I have been able to further my understanding within the realm of computer science. Immersing myself in the knowledge provided to sharpen my programming and problem-solving skills to new bounds. I have also expanded beyond my classes to truly indulge in my interests and fascination with AI and machine learning, gaining the invaluable opportunity to spend my second-year summer as a research assistant to help optimize and refine a newly created Long Short Term Memory (LSTM) neural network model called ‘E LSTM’, developed by my mentor, Ph.D. candidate Fernando Martinez Garcia. It was very exciting to be working on such a complex subject matter that had captivated my interest since the beginning of time, along with it being equally scary and overwhelming as a result of my lack of knowledge in the subject and its various intricacies. However, by the end of the four-month period, I had not only begun programming and training various LSTM models but had also begun to refine and optimize them. Eventually, all of it culminating with my continued work to refine and optimize the ‘E LSTM’ model. I am excited to continue my work on this incredible project into my third year.

Outside the lecture halls and research lab, I take pride in helping my computer science peers achieve their goals through my role as my year’s representative in the Computer Science Society. Where I represent my year’s student body on a department level, helping to get their voices to professors, faculty heads, and the overall university. I also greatly enjoy helping to provide innovators and problem-solvers like myself, as well as the future generation of STEM majors, with a platform to develop, learn, and showcase their skills through my role as a Technical Executive for DeltaHacks.

TL:DR: I am simply an aspiring computer scientist motivated by my love for the field and desire to make a positive impact on the landscape, which has since driven me to not only expand on my skills as a computer scientist but also become the best version of myself.
