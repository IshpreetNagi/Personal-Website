S.A.R.V: Search and Rescue Vehicles

Visit Devpost Page

Details

This project was intended for the ‘Brampton Library CANHack’ hackathon that took place during the summer of 2020. My group and I’s objective for this hackathon was to create a device that would better the lives of people and would tackle a real-world problem. Please refer to the link in the form of the button above to visit the Devpost page for this project to read further details and learn more regarding our final product.

Fundamentally, ‘S.A.R.V: Search and Rescue Vehicles’ is an automated drone storm system that would act as a search and rescue unit that could be deployed on beaches or really any place where a water-related natural disaster has occurred. The drones would fly together in a swarm formation, hunting for signs of stranded people using aids such as normal cameras, infrared night vision cameras for 24-hour searching, and an ultrasonic sonar that detects large irregularities along the bed of the body of water. Once the drones encounter a person, they would transmit their location to the control center that would be nearby, occupied by human rescue teams that could then be dispatched to rescue the stranded body. Moreover, the drone’s camera mounts would be modular thus allowing for the drone to be fitted to different types of cameras and devices such as a lifebuoy deploying box. The modular system allowed for the drones to be fitted with the best possible loadout for the situation and environment.
